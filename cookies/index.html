This Cookie Policy explains how AI Hub ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website at aihub.tools (the "Site").

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners. Cookies can be "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" cookies (stored for a set period).

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Site to function properly. They include:

  • Theme preference — Remembers your dark/light mode preference (stored in localStorage)
  • Session management — Maintains your session as you navigate the Site

Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics)

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use the Site. Google Analytics sets cookies to collect information such as:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • How you arrived at the Site (referral source)
  • Your browser type and operating system
  • Your approximate geographic location (country/city level)

This information is processed by Google in accordance with their Privacy Policy. You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Key Google Analytics cookies: _ga (2 years), _gid (24 hours), _gat (1 minute).

  • Interest-based advertising cookies
  • Conversion tracking cookies
  • Remarketing cookies

You can opt out of personalised advertising at Google Ads Settings or via the NAI opt-out page.

3. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View which cookies are set
  • Delete individual or all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies (note: this may affect Site functionality)

Browser-specific instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

4. Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" feature. We currently do not change our data collection practices in response to Do Not Track signals, as there is no industry standard for responding to such signals.
