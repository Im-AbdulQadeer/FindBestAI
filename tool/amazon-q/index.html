Best For Enterprises and AWS-heavy engineering teams that need a generative AI assistant grounded in their own business data and cloud environment

Amazon Q is AWS's enterprise generative AI assistant, split into Q Business for everyday work and Q Developer for engineering. It connects to your company's data through 40+ connectors, respects existing permissions, and runs inside the AWS trust boundary by default.

Visit Amazon Q → Browse More Tools Compare Tools

Overview

Q is AWS's answer to ChatGPT Enterprise and Microsoft Copilot. Q Business connects to SharePoint, Confluence, Salesforce, Jira, Google Drive and answers grounded in your own documents. Q Developer rolls in the old CodeWhisperer plus cloud aware chat for AWS engineers.

💰 Pricing Q Developer has a free tier and Pro at $19 per user per month. Q Business is $20 per user per month, with a $3 Lite tier for limited use.

Pros & Cons

✓ What We Like
✓Grounds answers in your own company documents through 40+ enterprise connectors
✓Respects existing identity and permission models, so users only see what they should
✓Data stays inside the AWS trust boundary, easing legal and compliance review
✓Q Developer is genuinely useful for AWS-heavy code, CDK, CloudFormation, and IAM
✓Pricing is competitive with ChatGPT Enterprise and Microsoft Copilot for Business
✗ Limitations
✗General reasoning still trails frontier models like GPT-5 and Claude Opus
✗Setup of connectors and permissions can be slow at large enterprises
✗Brand split between Q Business and Q Developer is confusing for new buyers
✗Less polished consumer experience than ChatGPT or Gemini
✗Heavy AWS bias, so non AWS shops get less value out of the box

Key Features

→Generative chat grounded in your own documents through SharePoint, Confluence, Salesforce, Jira, and 40+ connectors
→Permission aware answers that respect existing identity providers like Okta and Azure AD
→Q Developer chat for AWS code, CDK, CloudFormation, Terraform, and IAM policy authoring
→Inline code suggestions, security scans, and unit test generation in major IDEs
→Plugins for Salesforce, ServiceNow, Jira, and PagerDuty to take action, not just answer
→Runs inside the AWS trust boundary with private VPC, KMS, and audit logging
